Collected text:

Professor Allen's exams were consistently focused on lecture examples rather than the textbook. Multiple students note the midterm had several 'trick' questions that reused homework wording, so practicing problem sets was crucial. One review said: "If you can do all the homework and attend lectures, you'll survive the midterms — they reward pattern recognition more than deep theory." Several reviewers mentioned office hours were helpful but crowded the week before exams. A few commenters warned that solutions posted online sometimes included mistakes, so double-check your work with peers.

Another reviewer highlighted that the final was cumulative and heavily weighted (40% of the grade), and that previous years' finals were good predictors for the exam style. Students recommended forming study groups and making a list of recurring question templates from lectures.
